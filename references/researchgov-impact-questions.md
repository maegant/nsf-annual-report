# Research.gov Impact Page Questions

Use this reference when preparing the Research.gov Impact page. The Impact page asks how the project mattered during the reporting period. Answers should be short, simple-language but technically rich, and minimally overlapping. Project-specific impact facts belong in the active proposal folder's `nsf-report-context.md`.

## Style Rules

- Use simple language suitable for an intelligent lay audience while preserving technically accurate terms.
- Keep precise terms such as hybrid limit cycles, invariant sets, reachability analysis, contraction, trajectory optimization, robust motion synthesis, and contact-rich CPS when they are central to the answer.
- If a technical term may be unfamiliar, explain it briefly rather than replacing it with an inaccurate simplification.
- Make each answer as short as possible while still answering the field.
- A single sentence, two short sentences, or a short numbered list is fine.
- Avoid repeating the same products, publications, trainees, or broad-impact statements across fields.
- If there was no impact in a category during the reporting period, answer directly with "No impact to report for this period" or equivalent.
- Mention only impacts from the reporting period unless the prompt asks about likely future impact.

## Impact Summary Prompts

1. **Principal discipline(s)**: What is the impact on the development of the principal discipline(s) of the project?
   - Describe how findings, techniques, or products affected knowledge, theory, research methods, or teaching methods in the main field.
   - For NSF, focus on the impact on knowledge and technique; exact field boundaries are less important.

2. **Other disciplines**: What is the impact on other disciplines?
   - Describe whether the findings, techniques, or products are useful beyond the principal field.

3. **Human resources**: What is the impact on the development of human resources?
   - Describe opportunities for research, teaching, and mentoring in science and engineering.

4. **Teaching and educational experiences**: What was the impact on teaching and educational experiences?
   - Describe curriculum, classroom, student training, online resources, or educational materials, if any.

5. **Physical resources**: What is the impact on physical resources that form infrastructure?
   - Describe impacts on facilities, laboratories, instruments, testbeds, or physical research infrastructure.

6. **Institutional resources**: What is the impact on institutional resources that form infrastructure?
   - Describe impacts on policies, practices, programs, organizations, collaborations, or institutional capacity.

7. **Information resources**: What is the impact on information resources that form infrastructure?
   - Describe impacts on data, software, repositories, data services, preservation, or electronic access to research resources.

8. **Technology transfer**: What is the impact on technology transfer?
   - Describe impacts on commercial technology, public use, open-source use, invention disclosures, patents, licenses, or translation beyond the lab.

9. **Society beyond science and technology**: What is the impact on society beyond science and technology?
   - Describe effects on public welfare, safety, economy, education, decision making, policy, civic conditions, social conditions, or environmental conditions.

10. **Foreign-country budget percentage**: What percentage of the award's budget was spent in a foreign country?
   - Give the percentage spent in foreign countries during the reporting period.
   - If more than one foreign country was involved, identify the distribution by country.
   - If none, say "0%".
