# Research.gov Accomplishments Page Template

Use this reference for the Research.gov Accomplishments page. It contains generic prompts, field limits, and drafting guidance only. Project-specific variables and annual facts belong in the active proposal folder's `nsf-report-context.md`.

Draft final answers as briefly as possible. Avoid overlap between sections, and use simple language that remains technically rich. It is acceptable for an answer to be one sentence, two short sentences, or a numbered list if that fully answers the prompt.

Simplify syntax, not substance. Preserve technically accurate terms when they are central to the project. Briefly explain specialized terms in plain language if needed.

Do not leave bracketed notes in a Research.gov-ready draft. Ask the user for missing information or update `nsf-report-context.md` before drafting final responses.

# Accomplishments

Research.gov prompt: **Accomplishments - What was done? What was learned?**

Agency guidance shown on page:

- The agency uses this section to assess whether satisfactory progress was made during the reporting period.
- For NSF, provide accomplishments in the context of intellectual merit, broader impacts, and any program-specific review criteria.
- Include transformative outcomes or unanticipated discoveries when applicable.
- Significant changes in the project or direction may require prior written approval from the awarding agency grants official.

## Question 1. What are the major goals of the project?

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `project_title`
- `approved_goals`
- `major_research_threads_or_thrusts`
- `approved_changes`
- `milestones_planned`
- `milestones_completed`
- `percent_completion_by_goal_or_thrust`

Drafting guidance:

- Keep stable approved goals consistent year to year unless agency-approved changes occurred.
- Mention milestones, completion dates, or percent completion only when available.
- Keep this answer focused on approved goals, not current-year accomplishments.

## Question 2. What was accomplished under these goals and objectives?

Research.gov instruction: provide information for at least one of the four categories below.

### 2A. Major Activities

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `annual_activities`
- `annual_publications`
- `annual_software_data`
- `supported_personnel`
- `broadening_participation`

Drafting guidance:

- List the main activities completed during the reporting period.
- Do not repeat detailed results that belong in 2C.
- Do not include planned future work unless it explains current progress.

### 2B. Specific Objectives

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `annual_objectives`
- `milestones_completed`
- `milestones_partially_completed`
- `milestones_deferred`

Drafting guidance:

- State what was completed, partially completed, or deferred.
- Connect objectives to approved goals or thrusts.
- Keep this distinct from activities and results.

### 2C. Significant Results

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `annual_results`
- `main_results`
- `annual_publications`
- `annual_software_data`

Drafting guidance:

- Emphasize the most important technical, scientific, computational, experimental, or dissemination results.
- Explain why the results matter in simple but technically accurate language.
- Avoid re-listing every activity from 2A.

### 2D. Key outcomes or Other achievements

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `annual_outcomes`
- `supported_personnel`
- `mentoring_and_training`
- `broadening_participation`
- `annual_dissemination`

Drafting guidance:

- Summarize outcomes not already captured cleanly in 2A-2C.
- Include training, infrastructure, broadening participation, open-source, or collaboration outcomes when relevant.
- Keep the answer short.

## Question 3. What opportunities for training and professional development has the project provided?

Research.gov field limit: 8000 characters.

Use values from `nsf-report-context.md`:

- `supported_personnel`
- `mentoring_and_training`
- `professional_development`
- `undergraduate_research`

Drafting guidance:

- If graduate students or postdocs were supported, summarize mentoring activities.
- Include conferences, workshops, seminars, writing, software training, hardware training, and research presentations when relevant.
